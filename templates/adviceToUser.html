Monthly best, average and worst performing stocks are calculated based on the stocks previous performance in the month. Each stocks performance daily on the given month is calculated and each stock is compared with each other using median and standard deviation parameters.
Best stocks are the ones with the highest positive deviation from the mean while the worst stocks are the ones with the highest negative deviation. Average stocks are stocks that haven't deviated much from the mean. These stocks should be bought/sold at the start of the month to be sealed at the end of the month.
Daily best stock are calculated in a similar manner as the monthly best stocks but instead of using the monthly data, the data of the specific days that have occurred from the past are taken into account. For example: If tomorrow the eight of December and it is a Thursday, the stocks performance on the previous accounts it had on the eight of December and on the Thursdays of December are calculated. These data are then compared to each other to find the highest positive deviations from the mean of the stocks. These stock are then chosen as the best stocks to buy at the start of that day to sell at the end of that day.
