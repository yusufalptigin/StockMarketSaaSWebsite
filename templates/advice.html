In the stock market, moving averages are used to calculate the average price of a stock for the given number of days. MA10 can be translated into "Average price of the stock in the last 10 days. Moving averages are compared with each other to find out the average price of the given stock in different intervals. If a stock has a higher average in the last day than it has in the last 25 days, it can be said that the stock has had an uptrend in the last 10 days. This would give a buy signal. In the below tables, "Buy" and "Sell" signals are given by comparing each of MA10, MA25, MA50, and MA100 to MA200.
